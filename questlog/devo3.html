#3: Uncertainty

February 2019

There are no coincidences. Look around.

For truth feeds into commitment, and commitment is a declaration of further belief.

And another truth remains imminent: our all-loving and all-powerful Father works in our lives every second of every day.

And if we remain committed to this belief, then it must be true that any and all situations are part of a catalyst and a journey to bring Him more glory.

Yes, sometimes it does not make sense.

Sometimes it seems like there is absolutely no reason for this.

But remain stouthearted, o ye of little faith.

Do you not believe in the power of the Lord?

Do you not trust in the plans of the God Almighty?

For uncertainty resides with darkness, although in a slightly different manner.

Uncertainty in the world is natural. For who can know the future or predict the rest of their life?

If we were not uncertain about the rest of each day, I am convinced that a large amount of fear and worry would cease to exist.

And if we were not uncertain about the rest of each day, how much less effective would the Spirit be at providing You with spontaneous truths and opportunities?

For no eye has seen and no ear has heard what the Lord God has prepared for each and every one of us.

The Lord has promised great plans for His children.

But even so, it is not our responsibility to play prophet, or to try to determine God's plan for us.

Life is but a journey with one destination in the Father. All else is but part of this journey.

And so I remain uncertain about the world and its events, in order for God's glory and revelation to be even more of a pleasing surprise.

But uncertainty in the Father is a different matter.

Uncertainty in the Lord is doubt, and doubt is with the darkness.

If I proclaim belief in Christ but do not hand over my life to Him, I am a cheat and a liar.

If I proclaim Christ as the captain of my soul but refuse to trust Him, I am a coward.

Though I remain uncertain of the workings of this world, I must remain steadfast in the one truth: that all will turn out as it should be.

For there is no power on this Earth that can take me off the journey that the Lord has planned for me.

Especially not my own incompetence and unworthiness.

For Christ has made me a new creation in Him, and I am bound eternally to His fatherhood.

He is with me.

So there are no coincidences. Look around.

Uncertainty remains in the world, but in Christ there is everlasting hope.

So hold on, my friends.

Your faith will surely be rewarded soon.

If not now, then later.

If not later, then even later.

But trust that it will happen.

For this is the truth, and it will always remain.
