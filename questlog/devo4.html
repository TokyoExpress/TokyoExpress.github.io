#4: Peace

March 2019

Peace is nothing more than resting in the truth.

When there is anxiety in my heart and unrest in my soul, then I have no peace.

Peace comes with the trust that everything is alright.

Peace comes with the surefire knowledge that it is all going to turn out fine.

Peace is not something that can be found in the world.

For the world is full of uncertainty, of belief in disbelief, of distractions that turn away from the light.

The world wants to tell the believer that there are no absolutes, that everything can change in the fraction of a second.

That nothing is guaranteed, and that there is no hope for when the earth turns upside down.

The world wants to tell me that I’m still not good enough.

That I must still keep working to improve myself, that I must always participate in this race.

And so I’m running day and night, because the world tells me I should run.

What is at the end of this race is entirely up to me, it seems.

Because I choose how I run, I determine my own finish line.

I am responsible for my own fate. I am in control.

At least that’s what they’d like to have me believe.

Because the thing about control is that it’s a double-edged sword.

Being in control gives a sense of independence, a reason to be proud that I have it all together.

And everything is fine, until I mess up.

Until I fail.

Until I can do nothing to change the situation.

Then control becomes not a virtue but a curse, a screaming voice that takes away all the pride, all the joy. It’s my fault. It is my problem.

And because I am responsible, because I am the one in charge, because I am in control, I fall into despair. Because the world is right.

I’m not good enough to run this race, and I will never be good enough.

What is there left to do but to sit in my hopelessness?

For the world does not believe in anything else.

But I do.

I believe in the higher calling, the greater identity.

A force powerful enough to bring me out of the darkness, out of despair.

A God that I can trust with control of my life.

So I can find victory, even if I’ll never be good enough.

I continue to run, but no longer for my own sake.

It’s just something to pass the time while I draw closer to the Light.

I don’t care about what’s at the end of the race, because I know what awaits me after it all.

My fate has been determined, not of my own volition, but of the will of He who is greater than me.

I’ve already won, and I can do nothing to change it.

So I rejoice, for I am powerless to steer myself away from salvation.

I need not worry, for I am not in control. I can’t mess this up, even if I tried.

This is truth, and as we’ve learned, what is true remains true.

And because of this truth, I have living, eternal hope.

And where there is hope, there is peace.

What is there left to do but to rest in it?
