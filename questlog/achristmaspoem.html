A Christmas Poem

For My Lovely Significant Other

‘Twas the night before Christmas and all through the house,

Not a creature was stirring, not even a mouse.

The stockings were hung by the chimney with glee,

But not that type of stockings, unfortunately.

Ari was sleeping all snug in her bed,

While I lay here thinking of rhymes in my head.

For I saw someone parody this poem online,

And thought it’d be cute if I did it for mine.

It’s dreary without you amidst all the rain,

So let’s take a quick walk down memory lane.

I know this all happened quite recently,

But it’s romantic to reminisce, wouldn’t you agree?

The first time I saw you, I have to admit,

It was fun talking, but I didn’t think much of it.

Within a week though, I’m embarrassed to say

That I started planning far beyond today.

A grand scheme of sorts, with a much grander prize,

A girl with dazzling asymmetrical eyes.

So I got your number, but the hard part was next,

Spending all day thinking of what to text.

But slowly and surely you began to appear,

While I found myself doting and holding you dear.

Even still it seemed we were so far apart,

So I really hoped you wouldn’t break my heart.

But although I was afraid, I pressed on with hope,

Giving up on such a cute girl was definitely a nope.

I tried to be proactive and find things in common;

But I guess I really just had to ask for ramen.

I’ll never forget just how much brighter

My smile was when we pulled that all-nighter.

All through that weekend I was filled with delight,

My happy ending was finally in sight.

When you came back from your trip in LA,

Making you mine I just could not delay.

The first time we went out was indeed very sweet,

Walking up and down that dimly lit street.

I’m glad that being up there seeing the stars,

Is a moment that will only ever be ours.

From there we began to be more than friends,

Finding new excitement in the time we did spend.

Walking around campus; cooking good meals;

Me telling you you’d look perfect in heels.

With you not a single moment is dull,

My one and only partner in operations patrol.

Climbing tall buildings and spying on things,

Are just some of the stuff we can still do this spring.

I very much enjoyed our trip before break;

I’m pleased that you were able to partake.

Waiting for TWICE tickets down at the mall,

and pictures at Six Flags in the midst of it all.

And all sorts of other activities and sights;

I especially liked talking outside those nights.

I know we did some things quite out of order,

But there aren’t any changes I’d make to this quarter.

Being with you has been such a treat,

It’s so lovely to have a girlfriend this sweet.

I suppose we’re reaching the end of my tale,

For now together we begin to set sail.

To Monterey and Universal, or Seoul or NY,

(The ship is metaphorical, we’ll probably just fly.)

I’m so glad you’re with me to explore;

There’s so much to see, and things to do galore.

And though there are so many memories to come,

I’ll always treasure the things we have done.

For now I just wait for tomorrow and then,

A few more long days and I’ll see you again.

I always knew that for you I’d fall;

Merry Christmas to you, my most precious of all.
