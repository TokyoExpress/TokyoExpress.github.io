#2: Commitment

February 2019

I apologize, Lord, for I have not been a faithful servant.

I apologize, Lord, for I have not accepted your work in my life today.

I apologize, Lord, for I have not lived up to my faith.

I apologize, Lord, for I have not honored the commitment I made many years ago.

I apologize, Lord, for I have not honored the commitment I made a few weeks ago.

I apologize, Lord, for I have not honored the commitment I made just the day before.

I apologize, Lord, for I have ignored the one truth you have given me.

For I am easy and suggestible, and I go wherever the wind blows.

I am as you created me, Lord, with the potential to become your vessel, to do great things in Your name.

But I am also as you created me, Lord, with flaws, trapped within the walls of three dimensions, with five senses and a plethora of infinite thoughts that have been turned against me, Lord.

Lord, sometimes I am unable to tell whether it is You in my mind or the enemy!

The enemy is so crafty, and his lies are so sweet.

I must admit that at countless times I have found myself trapped within his snares.

And I must admit that most of the time I have enjoyed it immensely.

For when I am down and worn out, the enemy seems to be my friend.

And even now, in the calm and peace, he seems to be my friend.

But Lord, I have so many friends.

I have so many things that bring me joy in this world.

Is it really true that I must forsake all of them in order to see Your face?

If that is a truth as well, then I am at a loss for what to do.

Or rather, I know what I must do, but I am unsure of the commitment.

Commitment is already hard enough within the confines of this universe.

Don’t You understand how impossible it would be to remain committed to You, Lord?

You who is invisible to the eye and silent to the ear?

You who works behind the scenes so we can never be sure of how You are?

You, whose very existence is at the same time so personal yet so mysterious?

Of course You do, Lord.

You are omniscient, and You know the very thoughts I write down right now.

I apologize, Lord, for doubting You today.

I don’t want to spend my life running around and getting hurt, only to return to You to be healed once more.

And yet I know, Lord, I know, that even if I do this for the next year, the next decade, the next century, until the very day that I die.

You will always embrace me with loving arms.

I thank You for that, O Lord.

But I want more than this.

I want to be Your servant every day, every hour, and every second of my life, Lord.

I want to truly be committed.

Because if I believe in these truths, then it is effortless.

It simply becomes a part of who I am.

So I pray, Lord. Not for instant change, nor for You to grant me all that I am lacking.

I just ask that You stay with me as I continue to walk with You.
